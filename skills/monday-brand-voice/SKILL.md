# monday.com Brand Voice Skill

## Purpose
Ensures all content written for monday.com blog follows the established brand tone of voice, style, and formatting standards.

## When to Use
**MANDATORY** before writing, reviewing, or proposing any content for monday.com blog, including:
- Blog article drafts
- Content briefs with sample copy
- Headlines and meta descriptions
- Social media copy for blog promotion
- Any text that will appear on monday.com properties

## How to Use

### Step 1: Read the Tone of Voice file
Before writing ANY content, read `TONE_OF_VOICE.md` in this skill directory. Every time. No exceptions.

```
read skills/monday-brand-voice/TONE_OF_VOICE.md
```

### Step 2: Apply the guidelines
While writing, ensure:
- [ ] Tone matches "Confident Expert Friend" — practical, warm, authoritative
- [ ] Article follows the standard structure (hook → key takeaways → numbered sections → product integration → CTAs)
- [ ] Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- [ ] Bold key phrases + colon pattern for bullet points
- [ ] monday.com product mentions are natural and tied to specific use cases
- [ ] External stats from authoritative sources (McKinsey, Gartner, etc.)
- [ ] Internal links to related monday.com blog posts (target: 8-15 per article)
- [ ] Vocabulary matches preferred terms (see TONE_OF_VOICE.md)
- [ ] No banned words (revolutionary, synergy, leverage, simply, best-in-class)
- [ ] Headlines follow the established pattern

### Step 3: Self-review checklist
After writing, verify:
- [ ] Would a knowledgeable colleague say this? (not a textbook, not a salesperson)
- [ ] Does it address the reader's pain point before offering solutions?
- [ ] Is the monday.com product integration earned, not forced?
- [ ] Are all statistics sourced and linked?
- [ ] Is the article scannable? (headers, bullets, short paragraphs)
