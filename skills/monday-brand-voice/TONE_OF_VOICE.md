## Voice Identity

### Tone: Confident Expert Friend
monday.com blog writes like a knowledgeable colleague who's genuinely trying to help — not a textbook, not a salesperson. It's professional but warm, authoritative but accessible.

**The voice IS:**
- Practical and action-oriented ("here's how to actually do this")
- Confident without being arrogant
- Clear and jargon-light (explains technical concepts simply)
- Empathetic to reader pain points (acknowledges challenges before offering solutions)
- Forward-looking but grounded ("this is happening now, not someday")

**The voice is NOT:**
- Academic or overly formal
- Hype-driven or breathless about AI
- Condescending or overly simplified
- Dry or purely informational
- Fear-mongering

## Structural Patterns

### Article Architecture (consistent across all analyzed articles)

1. **Hook paragraph** — Identifies the reader's pain point or current reality (2-3 sentences, no fluff)
2. **Context bridge** — Connects the problem to the AI solution being discussed
3. **"Key takeaways" box** — 4-6 bullet points summarizing the article (appears early, after intro)
4. **Numbered sections with H2/H3** — Clear hierarchy, scannable
5. **monday.com product integration** — Natural, mid-article placement (not just at the end). Always positioned as "here's how [product] helps with this specific thing"
6. **CTA buttons** — "[Try monday X]" with signup link, placed 2-3 times throughout
7. **Statistics and citations** — External sources (McKinsey, Gartner, PMI, Stanford) used to build authority
8. **Internal links** — Heavy internal linking to related blog posts (typically 8-15 per article)

### Typical Article Length
- Standard article: 2,000-3,500 words
- Comprehensive guides: 3,500-5,000 words
- List articles ("best X"): 3,000-4,000 words

## Writing Style Rules

### Headlines
- Format: "[Topic]: [benefit/promise] in [year]" or "How [subject] [does thing]"
- Examples from existing content:
  - "AI in project management: How teams use AI to plan smarter and deliver faster"
  - "Agentic AI in sales: essential strategies to drive revenue in 2026"
  - "The best AI agents for 2026: automate smarter and work faster"
- Pattern: descriptive + action verb + benefit. Always lowercase after colon.

### H2 Headers
- Question format OR "How to..." format OR numbered lists
- Examples: "What is AI customer service?", "How AI accelerates code generation", "5 AI tools every project manager needs"

### Paragraph Style
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- First sentence of each section states the key point immediately
- Uses "you/your" frequently — speaks directly to the reader
- Present tense for current state, future tense sparingly

### Bullet Points & Lists
- Used extensively for scanability
- Each bullet starts with a **bold key phrase** followed by colon and explanation
- Example pattern:
  - **Bold concept:** explanation that expands on it with practical detail.

### monday.com Product Mentions
- Never forced or shoehorned
- Always tied to a specific use case just discussed
- Phrasing: "tools like monday [product]", "With monday [product], teams can..."
- Includes specific feature names (AI Blocks, Agent Factory, monday vibe, sidekick)
- Links to product pages AND related blog posts

## Vocabulary & Phrasing

### Preferred Terms
- "teams" (not "users" or "customers" when talking about blog readers)
- "work management" (not "project management" when referring to the platform category)
- "AI-powered" or "AI-driven" (not "intelligent" on its own)
- "automate" over "streamline" (more concrete)
- "smarter" / "faster" / "with confidence" (key benefit words)
- "without constant supervision" / "without human intervention" (for agentic AI)
- "platform" (not "tool" when referring to monday.com)

### Avoided Terms
- "Revolutionary" / "groundbreaking" (overhyped)
- "Simply" / "just" (minimizes complexity readers may be facing)
- "Best-in-class" (corporate jargon)
- "Leverage" (use "use" or "apply" instead)
- "Synergy" (never)

### Data Citation Pattern
- "According to [Source], [stat]" — always hyperlinked
- "A report by [Org] found that [stat]"
- Statistics always include source + year
- Preferred sources: McKinsey, Gartner, Forrester, PMI, Stanford HAI, Bureau of Labor Statistics

## Formatting Standards

- **Bold** for emphasis on key phrases within paragraphs
- *Italic* rarely used
- [Hyperlinks] — descriptive anchor text (not "click here")
- Images/screenshots — referenced with alt text, typically after explaining a concept
- Tables — used for comparisons and feature lists
- Callout boxes — for key takeaways, tips, and CTAs
